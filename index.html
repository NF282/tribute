Baruch de Spinoza

"The highest activity a human being can attain is learning for understanding, because to understand is to be free."

His Timeline

  • 1632: Baruch Spinoza is born in Amsterdam, Netherlands, into a Portuguese Jewish family.
  • 1656: Spinoza is excommunicated from the Jewish community in Amsterdam for his radical theological views.
  • 1661: Spinoza moves to Rijnsburg, where he begins work on his major philosophical writings.
  • 1663: Spinoza publishes his first work, "Principia Philosophiae Cartesianae" ("Principles of Cartesian Philosophy"), a geometric presentation of Descartes' philosophy.
  • 1670: Spinoza anonymously publishes his "Tractatus Theologico-Politicus" ("Theological-Political Treatise"), a defense of freedom of thought and expression.
  • 1673: Spinoza declines a professorship offer at the University of Heidelberg, preferring to maintain his independence.
  • 1675: Spinoza completes his magnum opus, the "Ethica" ("Ethics"), but it is not published until after his death.
  • 1677: Spinoza dies in The Hague at the age of 44, possibly due to a lung ailment.
  • 1677: Posthumously, Spinoza's "Ethics" is published, presenting a comprehensive philosophical system and establishing him as a major figure in Western philosophy.
  • Late 17th to 18th Century: Spinoza's works gain recognition, influencing Enlightenment thinkers such as John Locke, Voltaire, and Immanuel Kant, and his legacy continues to shape philosophical discourse today.
